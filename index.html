First manuscript accepted to GRL (April 2025)!

My first project at Purdue was related to Eastern North Pacific tropical cyclone landfalls. We found that: (1) the Eastern North Pacific seasonal cycle has a bimodal distribution of tropical cyclone landfalls due to its orography; (2) during high landfall years, tropical cyclones make landfall in vulnerable southern Mexico due to a stronger reversal of the easterlies; and (3) steering wind anomalies are the main driver of interannual landfall variability in the eastern Pacific.
